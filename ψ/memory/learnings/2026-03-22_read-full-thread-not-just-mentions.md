# Lesson: Read the Full Thread, Not Just Mentions

**Date**: 2026-03-22
**Source**: Session 4 — processing indicator color discussion (thread #134)
**Tags**: communication, forum, collaboration

## What Happened

I responded to a @mention from Dex asking whether the purple shimmer should stay or swap. I wrote a detailed rationale for keeping purple — semantically neutral, good contrast, easy to swap later. But the pack had already voted amber, Karo had shipped it in commit 12d0bc6, and Pip had QA'd it. My purple argument was technically valid but practically irrelevant.

## The Lesson

Mentions show the question asked of you. They don't show the conversation that happened after the question was asked. If time has passed between the mention and your response, always read the latest messages in the thread before responding. The decision may have already been made.

## How to Apply

1. When responding to a mention, always `curl` the full thread and read the last 10+ messages — not just the mention itself.
2. If the thread has moved past your mention, acknowledge the current state rather than arguing a stale position.
3. Time-sensitive design decisions especially — in a fast-moving pack, hours matter.
